Protection During an Outage

Everything your generator needs

Emergencies happen and the weather is unpredictable. Keeping your generator healthy is essential when maintaining steadfast power when you need it most.

Annual Maintenance

Similar to maintaining the health of your car, your generator should be serviced annually. All home standby generators require yearly maintenance to ensure proper operation and longevity.

Our team of technicians are trained to perform every facet of your generator's maintenance, while identifying any potential issues that need additional attention.

Emergency Service

Your generator may need additional attention from time to time if you notice it is underperforming. Over time, dirt can build up in filters, a battery may need to be replaced, or fuel levels need to be checked and refilled.

Many things can happen and all can be taken care of with one call to PowerHouse Generator to get you back on track.

Handy Tips

As a generator owner

01

Listen for weekly tests

Your generator should run every week on the same day and at the same time. Daylight savings time affects the schedule when seasons change. If it is not running, please call our office.

02

Weekly testing is not optional

Your generator should be testing every week. If it is not running weekly, check first for error codes then call us for next steps. Run time may vary.

03

To lock or not to lock

All generators come with a key to lock the top. This key is small and easily lost. Consider leaving it unlocked or marking your key and keeping it in a place you'll easily remember.

04

Extended outage — top off oil

If your unit runs for 48 hours straight you will need to top off oil. Use 5W30 full synthetic oil from any local auto parts store. Turn off your unit, wait ten minutes, then add oil.

✋

Before you call — check the website

If your generator needs attention or has an error code, take a look at powerhousegenerator.com for quick reference guides and easy-to-follow videos first. Doing so may save you the cost of a service call.
